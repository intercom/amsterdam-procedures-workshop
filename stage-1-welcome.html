What is a Fin Procedure?

A Fin Procedure is a structured, multi-step workflow that Fin follows to resolve a specific type of customer request. Unlike Guidance — which tells Fin what to say — a Procedure tells Fin what to do: gather data, make decisions, take actions.

Procedures are the right tool when:

  • Fin needs to look up real-time data to answer the customer
  • There are conditional decision points (if eligible → do X, else → do Y)
  • Fin needs to take an action on the customer's behalf
Think of it this way A Guidance note is a brief for Fin. A Procedure is a runbook. Both matter — but they solve different problems.

What we're building today

We'll build a Refund Request Procedure from scratch. A customer messages in asking for a refund. Fin will:

  1. Look up the customer's account
  2. Retrieve their recent orders
  3. Check refund eligibility for the relevant order
  4. Either process the refund or explain why it can't be done

By the end of the day you'll have built this yourself — and have a framework for mapping any of your own SOPs into a Procedure.

Anatomy of a Fin Procedure

A Procedure in Intercom is made up of:

ElementWhat it isMaps to your SOP
TriggerThe customer intent that starts the ProcedureThe customer message that kicks off the process
StepsActions Fin takes — each step calls a Data Connector or performs logicEach piece of data you need to gather
ConditionsIf/else branches based on data returnedYour decision points
OutcomesWhat Fin says or does at the end of each branchYour outcomes and agent actions

The refund use case mapped

Here's how today's Refund Request SOP translates directly into a Procedure:

  1. Trigger: Customer sends a refund-related message

    Fin recognises this intent and starts the Procedure automatically.

  2. Step 1 — Customer lookup: Fin calls the Customer Lookup connector

    Input: customer's email. Returns: customer_id, name.

  3. Step 2 — Order lookup: Fin calls the Order Lookup connector

    Input: customer_id. Returns: list of recent orders.

  4. Step 3 — Refund eligibility: Fin calls the Eligibility connector

    Input: order_id. Returns: eligible: true/false and a reason.

  5. Condition: Is eligible true?

    If yes → Step 4 (process refund). If no → explain policy and offer escalation.

  6. Step 4 — Process refund: Fin calls the Process Refund connector

    Input: order_id. Returns: confirmation_id. Fin confirms to the customer.

Data flows forward Notice how each step passes data to the next. The output of Customer Lookup (customer_id) becomes the input for Order Lookup. This chaining is what makes Procedures powerful.
